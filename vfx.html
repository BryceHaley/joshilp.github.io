Visual Effects

VFX Reel

The shots shown in this vfx reel are only a handful of the shots I've have worked on. The reel highlights interesting shots that involved Stereo Compositing, Rotoscoping, and Paint. The one area that cannot be visually shown is Extraction.

Rotoscoping

Roto involved a lot of tracing of objects in order to create precise masks. These often involved the use of various techniques to get accurate movement and precision.

Paint

Paint involved the removal of objects and artifacts in shots to prep the plate for compositing.

Stereo Compositing

Stereo Compositing is similar to normal compositing where shots were composited of various plates and passes to create a final shot. The difference is that each shot has depth, so objects have to be placed 'within' the screen in the z-axis. Though not too difficult to do with full CG shots, this is very difficult on live action shots. Backgrounds placed in depth relative to a foreground object will contain holes, as the shot now contains parallax. To patch these holes, extensive painting has to be done. This is often difficult, especially with organic objects, such as trees, water, people, etc.

Extraction

Extraction is the processes of taking a Nuke script and breaking it down for a stereo pipeline. This could mean recreating whole scripts, fixing major technical issues, writing python code, and developing tools and python scripts to solve problems. Working in this area is where I developed an appreciating for programming and computer science, which is why I persued a second degree in this field.
